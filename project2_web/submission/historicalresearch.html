History behind the fiction

I love history! I've been a reader all my life, but it wasn't until I took an elective class on Peter the Great in college that I came to understand what great stories the past holds. Did you know that Peter the Great was almost 7 feet tall at a time when the average man was 5' 4"? Or that he traveled to Holland while he was Czar and worked for months as an apprentice ship builder? Stranger than fiction--no?

So now I am playing out my long-time ambition to meld my love of great stories with my love of history by writing historical fiction. On this page I plan to provide some of my thoughts on history and fiction and point the reader towards some interesting information on both.

--Wayne Grant

Bringing stories to life on the pages of his books, Wayne has travelled across the world in pursuit of an authentic experience and historical accuracy in his books. Below, you'll see photos from his various trips, as well as iconic and inspirational locations central to his books.



Chester City Walls

Chester city walls consist of a defensive structure built to protect the city of Chester in Cheshire, England. Their construction was started by the Romans when they established the fortress of Deva Victrix between 70 and 80 AD.

Clocaenog Forest

The Clocaenog Forest is in west Denbighshire and east Conwy, Wales on Mynydd Hiraethog. It takes its name from the village of Clocaenog, near Ruthin.

Kinder Scout

Kinder Scout is a moorland plateau and national nature reserve in the Dark Peak of the Derbyshire Peak District in England. Part of the moor, at 636 metres (2,087 ft) above sea level, is the highest point in the Peak District, Derbyshire, and the East Midlands.

The River Dee, Chester

The River Dee (Welsh: Afon Dyfrdwy, Latin: Deva Fluvius) is a river in the United Kingdom. It flows through parts of both Wales and England, forming part of the border between the two countries.

The Keep, Peveril Castle

Peveril Castle (also Castleton Castle or Peak Castle) is a ruined 11th-century castle overlooking the village of Castleton in the English county of Derbyshire. It was the main settlement (or caput) of the feudal barony of William Peverel, known as the Honour of Peverel.

The Tower

A fortified tower (also defensive tower or castle tower or, in context, just tower) is one of the defensive structures used in fortifications, such as castles, along with curtain walls. Castle towers can have a variety of different shapes and fulfil different functions.
